HOW I USE PROMPTS
------------------------------------------------------------

Every generation uses two fields:

  Positive prompt  →  what you want
  Negative prompt  →  what you don't want

Keep prompts consistent across a batch. If pastel tones don't land, get more
literal with your color language — DiffusionBee responds better to specific
palette words than vague poetic ones.

------------------------------------------------------------
PROMPT TIPS FROM EXPERIENCE
------------------------------------------------------------

Pastel gradients:
  Use less contrast in the prompt or colors blow out. Be literal with
  palette names (cotton candy pink, baby blue, soft lavender).

Matte black themes:
  Always include "soft lighting" or results turn into pure black blobs.

Neon themes:
  Add "subtle glow" or highlights get too bright and clip.

Shorter prompts:
  Sometimes work better than long poetic ones. If output drifts, simplify.

Hallucination control:
  Add "no extra elements" or "no new objects." Make the prompt more literal
  and reduce creative modifiers.

Color fixes:
  Add "high color depth" or "rich gradients." Specify the palette directly.
